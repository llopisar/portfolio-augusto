A11Y Contrast Checker

A simple, responsive web tool to test color contrast ratios between background and text colors — following
WCAG accessibility standards.

Web tool to validate text and background color contrast according to accessibility standards (WCAG). It allows you to pick two colors, view the contrast ratio in real time, and check if they meet AA or AAA compliance levels. Includes dark mode with saved user preference, copy-to-clipboard for HEX values, and a clean, responsive UI.

Technologies: HTML, CSS, Vanilla JavaScript, LocalStorage.

What I learned: how to calculate contrast based on luminance, how to persist user preferences, and how to approach accessibility through both design and code.

About Me

I'm Augusto Llopis, a frontend developer with a background as a professional sous-chef — a journey that shaped my creativity, discipline, and love for building things from scratch. Originally from Argentina and now based in Portugal, I’ve always been drawn to design and technology. I studied graphic design for three years after high school, and even though I didn’t complete the degree, design has remained a core part of how I think and build. Today, I channel that same passion into creating thoughtful, user-centered digital experiences.

From Kitchen to Code

Before tech, I spent over six years working in kitchens — leading teams, solving problems under pressure, and constantly iterating to get things just right. During that time, I also built websites for restaurants, contributed to online forums, and learned design tools on my own.
In 2023, I made a clear decision: to transition fully into frontend development and shape a creative career where design, code, and AI come together. I moved to Portugal and have been immersed in learning and building ever since.

My Frontend Journey

In the past year, I’ve been building a strong foundation in frontend development — combining my visual design background with a growing focus on artificial intelligence. I follow structured learning roadmaps, take online courses (Platzi), and use tools like ChatGPT as my daily code mentor.
I’m especially interested in how AI can enhance the creative and technical sides of frontend — from prototyping and debugging, to dynamic content and accessibility. I've participated in early betas of image generation models and love exploring how prompt engineering influences results, even if I don’t fully understand the code behind it (yet).

I'm now looking for my first opportunity as a frontend developer where I can grow with a team, contribute with both design and development, and keep learning how AI and the web can creatively evolve together.
